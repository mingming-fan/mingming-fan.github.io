The Intelligent and Inclusive Interaction (Triple-Eye) Group designs, develops, and evaluates interactive, intelligent, and inclusive computational systems and methods to help identify and address user experience (UX) and accessibility problems in the following three directions:

  • Human-AI Collaboration for User Experience (UX) Analysis
    • Understanding UX Problems via users' Subtle Behavioral Signals
      e.g., What are the subtle verbalization and speech patterns that indicate UX problems? (CHI'21, TOCHI'19)
    • Building AI Models to Detect UX UX Problems
      e.g., How could we better build AI models to detect UX problems? (TiiS'20)
    • Designing AI-assisted Interactive Systems to Support UX Evaluators
      e.g., How to design AI-assisted interactive systems to assist UX practitioners with analysis? (TVCG'20)
  • Aging and Accessibility
    • Understanding Accessibility Challenges via User-Centered Design
      e.g., What are the perceptions of Accessibility and Assistive Technology use in China? (CHI'21)
      e.g., Could instructions be designed user-friendly to support older adults to learn technologies? (TACCESS'18)
      e.g., Could mobile and wearable technology be used to design a memory-aid for older adults? (IMWUT'19)
    • Designing Assistive Technologies using AI & ubiquitous computing
      e.g., Could eyelid gestures be used by people with motor impairments to interact with mobile devices? (ASSETS'20)
      e.g., How to design an easy-to-learn gesture-based text-entry method for people with visual impairments? (ASSETS'17)
  • Mobile, Wearable, and Ubiquitous Sensing & Interaction Techniques
    • Virtual Reality (VR) Interaction Techniques
      e.g., how to make the selection of occluded and distant objects in VR easier ? (CHI'21)
      e.g., Could we enhance VR users' experiences by providing them with non-visual sensation (e.g., wetness)? (CHI'20)
    • Novel Sensing and Interaction Techniques
      e.g., Could the content level inside a content be inferred unobstrustively and continuously? (UbiComp'15)
      e.g., Could we make a wearable device smart enough to understand the type of space that its wearer is in? (ISWC'14)
      e.g., How to redesign a hierarchical list to improve its navigation efficiency on mobile devices? (CHI'19)

The Triple-Eye Group is led by Prof. Mingming Fan in the iSchool of the Golisano College of Computing and Information Sciences at Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT), Rochester, New York, USA.
