Prospective Ph.D. Students: Our group is looking for talented students who want to pursue a Ph.D. in HCI, VR/AR, Accessibility, or Human-AI Collaboration. You should have strong skills in at least two of the following: 1) programming (e.g., web, mobile, VR/AR, AI/ML), 2) design (e.g., product, interaction), 3) hardware prototyping, 4) quantitative research methods (e.g., experimental design, statistical analysis), or 5) qualitative research methods (e.g., interview, field study, thematic analysis). If you think you are a good fit, please contact Prof. Fan & apply Ph.D. in Computational media & Arts.

Current HKUST Students: If you're interested in HCI, VR/AR, Accessibility, or Human-AI Collaboration and are eager to use advanced technology (e.g., AI, IoT, Visualization, VR/AR) to tackle human-centered, social and societal challenges (e.g., aging, disabilities, human-AI interaction, sustainability, UX), we should talk. Please contact Prof. Fan to discuss potential projects.

RA/Internship Opportunities: If you're interested in working with me on challenging and interesting HCI and accessiblity problems, I am happy to discuss potential opportunities. We publish our work at top-tier venues in HCI and Accessibility. If you work hard and make solid contributions, you will be part of the submissions as a co-author and receive recommendation letters when you apply for graduate schools.
