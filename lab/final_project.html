Final Project

In this assignment, you will work in groups to complete a project that uses one of the two taught sensing technologies (i.e., capacitive sensing and active acoustic sensing), Photon's internet connectivity, Prototyping skills (3D printing or Laser Cutting), and Web Programming (JavaScript/HTML/CSS) to address an interesting problem.

Learning objectives

The goal of this assignment is to work in groups to solve a relatively complex IoT project that involving using one of the taught sensing technologies and Photon's internet connectivity to solve an interesting problem./p>

You team's final project must:

  1. Use one of the two taught sensing technologies: capacitive sensing or active acoustic sensing (Note that temperature sensor and passive infrared sensor are too simple to be qualified for the final project. However, if your team has an idea of using these sensors in a comprehensive way, send me a slack message to discuss.)
  2. Use Photon's Internet Connectivity: your team's final prototype must be able to send sensed data to a webserver over Internet
  3. Visualize sensed data
  4. Contain a reasonable logic to process and leverage the sensed data in real time
  5. Send an alert (e.g., an email, a slack message, a short message) when certain conditions are met. For example, for the active acoustic sensing or the capacitive sensing, an alert could be generated when your prototype detects a specific type of space (e.g., certain part of the classroom) or a specific property of an object (e.g., less than 1/3 detergent is left in a laundry detergent bottle)
  6. Be a stand-alone unit (that can be easily moved around) and look reasonably pleasing. For example, your team's protype can be housed in a 3D-printed or laser-cutted housing and powered by a battery unit.
  7. Must be able to be demonstrated live in class

Deliverables

See the notes below for details on each deliverable, including how to turn them in, as well as grading criteria.

  1. A project proposal
  2. Documentation in the form of an Instructable explaining how to recreate your project
  3. Github Repository to store your team project code
  4. A well-produced video demonstrating your project.
  5. Weekly progress update about your team's project and your contributions.
  6. A final in-class presentation and live-demo of your team's project

What to do

There are multiple steps for this assignment, with deliverable dates!

Groups

This is a group project! You will work in a group of two or three. I will assign groups based on your input.

You will, by the date below (see Deliverables), send me a private Slack message that contains:

  • A self-evaluation of the following skills: Photon Circult Construction, Photon Internet Connectivity, Sensing Technology Understanding, JavaScript/CSS/HTML Programming and Visualization, and Writing
  • A list of the people in the class whom you particularly want to work with, and reasons why.
  • (Optional) A list of the people in the class whom you particularly do not want to work with, and reasons why.
  • Or, if you do not have a preference of people that you do or don’t want to work with, send me a message stating this.

I will form groups based on your feedback and aim to balance your team's skillsets.

Proposal

Your team needs to turn in a proposal for your project. It should be a well-written document, in PDF format, 2–3 pages long

Use this template that I have prepared to help your team think and construct your proposal.

Weekly Update

After your team is formed, each Thursday by noon, you should compose a slack message answering the following questions:

  • What have you personally done technically on the project during the preceding week?
  • What design contributions have you personally made to the project during the preceding week?
  • What has your team mate (or mates) done technically and design-wise on the project during the preceding week?
  • What problems are you having with
    • your team,
    • your project,
    • technology,
    • anything else?

Code, Documentation, and Video

Your team needs to organize your code clearly with sufficient comments, and then upload it github.

In addition, your team needs to create documentation in the form of an Instructable explaining how to recreate your project. Include as many high-quality images as needed to demonstrate how to complete each step and what the unit would look like at each step. Also include code snippets and the github repository link(s) at appropriate in your Documentation. See how-to-make-good-instructables.

Finally, you team needs to create a well-produced video, approximately one minute or two in length, to demonstrate the key components/features of your project and how it works. Tips: You can use video prototyping approach to have actors and narratives to help audience understand the problem that your team aims to tackle and how your project actually solves it.

Final Project Presentation, Live Demo, and Q & A

Your team will give a presentation and a live-demo about your project in class. Each team has up to 15 minutes for the presenttion and live-demo. Afterward, each team has a Q&A session that is up to 5 minutes. Therefore, reherse your presentation and live-demo well in advance and prepare for potential questions.

All students are required to attend all the Final Project Presentation, Live Demo, and Q & A sessions. When you are not a presenter, you should be actively listening to other groups' presentations and ask questions.

Grading

This project is worth 40% of your final grade. That amount will be divided as follows:

  • Project proposal (10%):
    • Contains the requested information (8%)
    • Is well-written, organized, and grammatical (2%)
  • Group participation (15%)
    • You turn in the required individual report by noon every Thursday (5%)
    • You have participated fully as part of your group (10%)
  • Prototype, Code, Documentation, and Video (50%)
    • Prototype Design (20%)
      • Functionality (e.g., completeness and complexity) (15%)
      • Design and Aesthetics (5%)
    • Code (10%)
      • Completeness (5%)
      • Understanability (e.g., comments) (5%)
    • Documentation (12%)
    • Video (8%)
  • Final Project Presentation, Live Demo, and Q&A (25%)
    • Presentation & Live Demo (20%)
      • Presentation (10%)
      • Live Demo (10%)
    • Q&A (5%)
