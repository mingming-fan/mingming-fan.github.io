Best Paper Awards in Human-Computer Interaction

Background: When I recently started my career in the Computational Media and Arts (CMA) Thrust at HKUST. I realized that our students had incredibly diverse academic backgrounds from a wide spectrum of subfields of science, engineering, art, and design. While they, as many other aspiring students and scholars, are eager to explore the beauty of the field of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), they are often overwhelmed by the daunting amount of published papers in the field. While the papers that won Best Paper Awards are not necessarily ones that have most significant impacts to the field and our societies, they nevertheless provide some examples, in terms of writing, ways of executing research, or research topics, for new comers to study and also for insiders to reflect upon. As a result, in my "spare" time, I search the relevant information and curate a list of recently published papers that won Best Paper Awards in the field of HCI.

The list contains the most recently published Best Papers in the following highly reputable HCI Conferences: CHI, UbiComp/ISWC/Pervasive/IMWUT, CSCW/PACMHCI, ASSETS, UIST, IUI, DIS

If you are interested in Best Paper Awards in the field of Visualization, please check out this following list that I curate: Best Paper Awards in Visualization.

If you are interested in Best Paper Awards in the whole field of Computer Science (CS), Jeff Huang maintains an awesome list.
