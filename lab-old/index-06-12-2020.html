The Intelligent and Inclusive Interaction (Triple-Eye) Group designs, develops, and evaluates interactive intelligent solutions to detect and address user experience (UX) and accessiblity problems and to create novel and inclusive UX in the following three directions:

  • AI-assisted Interactive User Experience (UX) Analysis Methods
    • Understanding Users' Experiences via users' Subtle Behavioral Signals
      e.g., Do subtle patterns in users' verbalization and speech indicate usability problems? (TOCHI'19)
    • Designing AI-assisted Interactive Systems to Support UX Analysis
      e.g., How to design AI-assisted interactive systems to assist UX practitioners with analysis? (TVCG'19)
  • Assistive Technology
    • Understanding Accessibility Challenges via User-Centered Desgin methods (UCD)
      e.g., How to design user-friendly instructions for older adults? (TACCESS'18)
      e.g., Can wearable technology be designed as memory-aid for older adults? (IMWUT'19)
    • Designing Assistive Technology using AI, Mobile, Wearable, VR and AR Technology
      e.g., Can touch-screen text-entry be more accessible for people with visual impairments? (ASSETS'17)
  • Human-Sensing Technologies and Applications
    • Context Sensing and Understanding
      e.g., Can wearable devices understand the type of space that the wearer is in? (ISWC'14)
      e.g., Can IoT devices unobstrusively infer the content level inside a container? (UbiComp'15)
    • Novel Interaction Techniques to Provide New UX
      e.g., Can hierarchical list navigation on mobile devices be improved? (CHI'19)
      e.g., How to enrich the UX of virtual reality (VR) users by providing wetness sensation? (CHI'20)

The Triple-Eye Group is led by Prof. Mingming Fan in the iSchool of the Golisano College of Computing and Information Sciences at Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT).
